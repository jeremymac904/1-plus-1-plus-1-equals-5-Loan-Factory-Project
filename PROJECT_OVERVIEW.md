# Project Overview

## The name

**1+1+1=5** — the working name for the Loan Factory Team Marketing Enhancement Program.

The math is intentional. One LO marketing alone gets 1 unit of reach. Three LOs marketing alone get 3 units. Three LOs marketing under a unified team brand with shared infrastructure get 5+ units. The non-linear return comes from shared content, shared partner pools, shared compliance review, and a brand the audience can actually remember.

## What we're building

A 90-day pilot — launching **June 1, 2026** — that gives Loan Factory loan officers a way to grow as **collaborative marketing teams**:

- **Language-based** (Spanish/Latino, Vietnamese, Russian, Korean)
- **Niche-based** (Veteran/Military, Investor/DSCR, future expansion)
- **Regional** (under existing or new team brands)
- **Audience-based** (First-Time Buyer, HELOC, Self-Employed, etc., post-pilot)

Each team operates with one Team Leader, minimum 3 participating LOs (no cap), LOs across at least 2 cities or states, a shared brand kit, and a standard publishing cadence.

## What problem this solves

| Problem | What team marketing changes |
| --- | --- |
| Solo LO marketing fails — no time, no consistency, no reach | Spreads the production load, standardizes the infrastructure, amplifies the brand |
| Vietnamese, Spanish, Russian, Korean communities underserved | Gives in-language LOs a unified marketing umbrella in their community |
| Veteran and Investor niches need specialized content | Niche divisions concentrate audience-specific content and partner outreach |
| LO recruiting expensive and unfocused | Strong team brands attract LOs organically; recruiting cost per LO drops |
| Marketing pod can't scale to 200 individual LOs | One Marketing pod can support 10–20 teams with template-level approval |

## What we're NOT building

- Not a separate company per language or niche
- Not new software (we wire existing systems together)
- Not a long phased enterprise rollout
- Not a brand-new comp structure (parallel work, not gating)
- Not bound to a 2-team cap

## The infrastructure already exists

- **Ally** — social scheduling per team
- **Loan Factory portal — Email Campaign / Send Email** — newsletters + Realtor outreach
- **Loan Factory portal — Email Drip** — new-contact nurture
- **Loan Factory IQ** — monthly homeowner equity / market reports
- **Google Business Profile** — team-level GBP per market
- **Gemini / NotebookLM** — AI drafting layer
- **Loan Factory CRM** — application attribution to teams

We're connecting them, not rebuilding them.

## The compliance position

- $2,000 Lowest Rate and Fee Guarantee disclosure baked into every applicable template
- Template-level compliance approval wherever possible
- Human-in-the-loop review on all published content
- No autopublish on any channel
- Bilingual reviewers for in-language divisions

## What success looks like at Day 90

- 4–10 active pilot teams across multiple divisions
- 70%+ of teams green on cadence compliance
- At least one team showing clear application growth attributable to team marketing
- Documented playbook ready to scale to 20+ teams
- Marketing + TERA pod capacity documented and predictable
- Team Leader comp model finalized in parallel (Andre + Edward + Finance)
- Capacity-based intake open broadly on September 1

## Why this matters now

Loan Factory is investing in growth. Andre's Teams revamp is in flight. Marketing is building capacity. TERA is unifying platforms. The AI Training program is shipping. This pilot is the operational layer that connects all of that into one growth engine.

If we launch June 1 and learn for 90 days, we walk into Q4 with a proven playbook and a real path to scaling teams across the country.

If we wait until everything is perfect, we lose 90 days of compounding.
